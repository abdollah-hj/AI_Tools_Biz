How to Create a Strong Password

A strong password is your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. Here are some tips for creating and managing secure passwords:

Password Best Practices

  • Use long passwords - Aim for at least 12-16 characters. Longer passwords are generally more secure.
  • Mix character types - Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid personal information - Don't use names, birthdays, or other personal details that could be easily guessed.
  • Don't reuse passwords - Use a unique password for each account, especially for important ones like email and banking.
  • Use a password manager - Consider using a password manager to securely store and generate complex passwords.
  • Change passwords periodically - Update your passwords regularly, especially for critical accounts.
